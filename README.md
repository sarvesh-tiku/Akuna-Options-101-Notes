# Akuna Capital Options 101 — Personal Notes

Personal textbook-style notes covering introductory options market making concepts, based on Akuna Capital's Options 101 program.


These notes are written the way I wish the material had been presented to me the first time — precise definitions, worked examples, and the trading intuition behind each concept, not just the formulas.

## Contents

| Chapter | Topic |
|---|---|
| 1–4 | Market terminology: bid/offer, spread, size, hedging, microstructure |
| 5–6 | Order types (IOC, FOK, GTC, OCO) and market participants |
| 7–8 | Options-specific terms and how the trading floor operates |
| 9–10 | Forwards, futures, options on futures, and forward pricing |
| 11–13 | Listed options, contract specs, multipliers, edge vs. cash, futures ladders |
| 14 | Payoff diagrams: calls, puts, spreads, butterflies, straddles, put-call parity |
| 15 | Delta — price sensitivity, hedge ratio, ITM probability, covered trades |
| 16 | Gamma — curvature, re-hedging, gamma scalping, cash gamma |
| 17 | Theta — time decay, the gamma-theta tradeoff, efficiency benchmarks |
| 18–19 | Volatility — historical vs. implied, vega, rho, boxes |


## A Note on the Material

Options market making can feel abstract when studied from papers and problem sets alone. These notes try to bridge that gap — every definition is followed by the trading intuition behind it, and every Greek is explained not just as a formula but as something a desk actually uses.

The gamma-theta tradeoff, the role of theoretical values, the difference between realized and implied volatility, the box as a financing instrument — these are not just exam topics. They are how market makers think.
